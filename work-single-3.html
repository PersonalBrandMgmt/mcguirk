Literature

Joel Greenblatt

I first heard of Joel Greenblatt when I was listening to a Bloomberg Masters in Business podcast episode featuring him. Greenblatt had incredible success running his own hedge fund Gotham Capital with annualized returns of 40% from the late 80s to the mid 90s and had a way of teaching on the podcast that instantly clicked with me. As simple as it sounds (and it’s literally the very basics of pricing) he said that the true value (different from price) of a stock is just the sum of the future discounted cash flows from now until forever, and that while the price of the an individual security may fluctuate throughout a year, the underlying aspects of the business are staying relatively the same. I enjoyed reading every single page of the three books I could find by Mr. Greenblatt. The way he was able to look at individual companies, see inefficiencies in the market, and make incredible returns for his investors was absolutely fascinating. His earliest and most complicated book of the three, You Can be a Stock Market Genius, details different kinds of special situations investing such as LEAPS, risk arb, merger arb, and others where he was able to find undervalued companies that most people have heard of and would recognize and make insane returns on them.

For me personally, that’s the kind of investor I want to be and whose investment philosophy conforms greatly with my own.
